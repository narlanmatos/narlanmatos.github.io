---

poem_id: calendario-en

title: "Calendar"
  
---

<div class="poem"> 
<br><br>

it’s right to forget about March<br>

so that April can finally arrive<br>

to lie beneath this January shade<br>

so that the abyss of June can disappear<br>
<br><br>

whose face is this behind the ivy?<br>

pale and white the moon’s ether light<br>

lay above the lilies of absence and chimera<br>
<br><br>

still they remain, the grasses of September<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;and the azaleas of afternoon<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;and the latitudes of silence<br>
<br><br>

it’s not death I search for, amiga,<br>

when the breeze brings your words<br>

when you offer me the scent of your skin<br>

and the Milky Way is suddenly reborn<br>

calmly among the meadow’s wild roses<br>

or when you open the immense petals<br>

of your clear and bright smile (a lily?)<br>

to the night of my being<br>

</div>
